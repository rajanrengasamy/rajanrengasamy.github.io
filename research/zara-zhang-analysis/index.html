She builds AI products at ByteDance during the day and creates content on the side. That constraint is what makes her approach interesting and directly relevant to our situation.

Her Products

TLDW (tldw.us)

A tool for learning from long YouTube videos. Not a text summarizer. She specifically rejects text summaries as lossy compression. Instead, TLDW creates personalized highlight reels: the AI identifies the highest-signal segments of a video and marks them on the progress bar. You click, you watch the original clip. No watering down.

  • Personalized highlights (type "future of education" and it surfaces relevant segments)
  • In-context term explanation (select jargon, AI explains using the video's context)
  • Note-taking integrated with transcript
  • Chat with the video transcript
  • Personal learning hub across all watched videos

Built on nights and weekends with 2 collaborators. Open-sourced.

follow-builders (GitHub, 227 ⭐)

An automated pipeline that monitors top AI builders on X and YouTube podcasts, then remixes their content into digestible summaries. JavaScript. This is essentially a simpler version of our Mercury pipeline: signal ingestion, filtering, and packaging.

Core Philosophy

"Follow Builders, Not Influencers"

Zara draws an explicit line:

  • Builders: People whose day job is making AI products. Founders, PMs, engineers at model companies or AI startups. They post about their process as a side activity.
  • Influencers: People whose full-time job is posting content. Revenue from sponsorships. You never know if they genuinely use what they promote.

Her method: identify AI products she admires → find who built them → follow them on X → watch ALL their YouTube interviews/talks/podcasts → package insights.

"The specific thing I do is use YouTube as a search engine for builder insights. I put a builder's name in the YouTube search bar and watch basically all of their video podcasts, interviews, or speeches."

"Content Is the Product"

She argues it's better to build traffic first, then make a product, rather than the reverse. Content has a shorter feedback loop: publish a piece, see reactions the same day. Build a feature, wait weeks to know if users care.

"Build for One"

"We used to build for the world. Now, we can build for the self. That shift might not make headlines. But it might just change how we think, work, and live — one creation at a time."

AI has collapsed the cost of building so far that creating tools for an audience of one (yourself) is now rational. TLDW started as her own pain point. follow-builders started as her own curation process. The products serve her first, then serve others.

Her Growth Playbook (Deconstructed)

  1. Tools as audience engines. TLDW and follow-builders are real, working tools. They drive organic attention because people USE them. 227 GitHub stars for a relatively simple curation pipeline. The tool IS the marketing.
  2. Builder curation as value creation. She finds builders, studies their work deeply, and packages insights. This creates reciprocal attention: builders notice when someone thoughtfully amplifies their work.
  3. Side project credibility. ByteDance gives her institutional credibility. Side projects give her builder credibility. The combination is powerful.
  4. Multi-platform presence. X for quick insights. Substack for long-form. YouTube for video. GitHub for developer attention. Each platform serves a different audience entry point.
  5. Authentic constraint narrative. She's building on nights and weekends. Trilingual and cross-cultural. Psychology degree, not CS. These constraints become her story.

What We Should Steal

Action 1
Talk about our pipeline as a product. Her simpler follow-builders repo got 227 stars. Our pipeline is 10x more sophisticated: 7 sensors, LLM filtering with Haiku/Sonnet/Opus tiering, editorial oracle (Gate 0 + Gate 1), autonomous publishing across 6 daily windows, escalation management, ARC overnight builds. We never frame this as something others could learn from. We should.
Action 2
Open-source pipeline components. Signal scanner, auto-publisher, escalation manager, ARC overnight build system. Any of these as standalone tools would generate stars and attention. Pick one, clean it up, ship it as a public repo this month.
Action 3
More "here's what I built" posts. We're currently ~60% commentary, ~25% curated shares, ~15% builder stories. Zara inverts this. She leads with what she built, then contextualizes. Flip to 60% builder stories, 25% curated, 15% commentary.
Action 4
Curate builders, don't just comment on news. "I found 3 builders this week doing multi-agent orchestration differently" is more valuable than "OpenAI released X." One builder curation post per week.
Action 5
Push VidLens MCP as our TLDW. VidLens MCP is a better, more comprehensive version of what Zara built. 41 tools vs her focused highlight reels. Zero-config. Three-tier fallback. Visual search. It's our audience engine — push it.

The Biggest Gap

We have MORE receipts than Zara. We just don't show them.

5 agents across 3 machines. 60+ automated crons. ARC overnight R&D pipeline. VidLens MCP (41 tools, zero config). Real production infrastructure running 24/7. But we talk about our building maybe 15% of the time. She talks about hers 70% of the time.

The follower growth will follow the usefulness. She proved that.
